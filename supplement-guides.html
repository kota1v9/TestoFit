Supplement Guides

Vitamin D

Vitamin D is essential for healthy testosterone production and overall immune function. It helps regulate hormones and supports bone health. Many people are deficient, especially in winter or if they spend little time outdoors. Taking a high-quality Vitamin D supplement can boost your testosterone and mood.

How to use: 2000–5000 IU daily is common, but testing your levels is best for personalized dosing.

Safety: Vitamin D is fat-soluble, so avoid mega doses without supervision to prevent toxicity.

Zinc

Zinc is a vital mineral for testosterone synthesis, immune health, and wound healing. Deficiency in zinc can lead to lowered testosterone and increased risk of infections. Supplementing with zinc can restore levels and promote better hormone balance.

How to use: Typical doses are 15–30 mg daily, ideally with food to improve absorption.

Safety: Avoid excessive intake above 40 mg/day long term as it may cause copper deficiency.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids (Fish Oil)

Omega-3s help reduce chronic inflammation that can negatively impact testosterone levels. They also improve heart and brain health, enhancing blood flow and metabolic function. Adding a quality fish oil supplement supports hormonal balance and overall wellness.

How to use: 1000–3000 mg of combined EPA and DHA daily is common for health benefits.

Safety: Fish oil is generally safe; just watch for blood thinning effects if on medication.

Garlic Extract

Garlic is known for boosting testosterone by lowering cortisol, the stress hormone that inhibits testosterone production. It also supports heart health and improves circulation. Garlic supplements can be an easy way to harness these benefits without the strong taste.

How to use: Typical doses are 600–1200 mg daily, divided into two doses.

Safety: Garlic can thin blood, so consult a doctor if you take blood-thinners or have surgery planned.
